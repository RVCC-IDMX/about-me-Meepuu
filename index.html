CSS Animation

Ive choosen CSS Animations because it allows animations with keyframes without using JS or RIP, Flash.

It has 10 properties you can use, @keyframes, animation, animation-delay, animation-duration, animation-direction, animation-fill-mode, animation-iteration-count, animation-name, animation-playstate, animation-timing-function.

You can do a bunch of things with this, you can have a fun little minigame using the animation on your page, show a small animation you've made with it, or use it to showcase some crazy stuff you've come up with and are now showing others.

in the case you have some really high animations or loops, the cpu usage is going to be really heavy on your pc, so when doing said things make sure whatever you're adding to the page doesnt take a load from your pc or is really strainous to run because theres a chance your client or targetted party's PC might not be able to keep up.

You've probably used aniamtion somewhere in a project for webdev and havent even noticed it, the place that it probably was used was in the Nav Bar making because if you hover over my nav bar, it has a small animation at the top of it and the hover turns my text white instead of red.

I think its super neat that you can do this without having to be dependant on JS or again, RIP FLASH. Its usage all comes from how you make it work in css, the usage is unlimited or well limited until your PC explodes.
